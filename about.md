# Pile

Pile is a semi-realistic nuclear reactor simulator that runs in your browser. Three reactor types share a common physics engine: a 1D axial point-kinetics model with six delayed-neutron precursor groups per axial node, fuel/clad/coolant thermal feedback with type-specific coefficients, iodine-135 to xenon-135 dynamics on the real 6.7-hour and 9.2-hour timescales, burnup-dependent kinetics and excess reactivity, ANS-5.1 decay heat, IAPWS-IF97 saturation temperatures, and coupled plant systems from core heat source through heat sink and grid demand. The model is intentionally lumped rather than CFD- or RELAP-grade, but the important state variables are coupled rather than scripted.

The three reactor types are a Westinghouse-class pressurized water reactor at 3411 MWth, a graphite-moderated RBMK-1000 at 3200 MWth with boiling water in pressure tubes and a direct turbine cycle, and a molten salt reactor modeled at MSRE scale with fuel dissolved in FLiBe. The default state is hot full-power equilibrium: delayed precursors, iodine, xenon, decay heat, burnup, and RBMK operating void are populated and then snapshotted as reactivity references so the initial reactor is critical by construction without pretending those inventories are absent. The PWR is strongly negative-feedback; its four-loop reference plant is collapsed to one representative primary loop and steam generator in the interactive model. The RBMK has graphite-temperature feedback, a low-power positive void coefficient, steam quality separated from void fraction, graphite-tipped rods whose lower section can add positive reactivity during insertion, and two modeled plant-side loops that each collapse four physical main circulation pumps into one running and one standby pump. The MSR has very strong fuel-temperature feedback, circulating delayed-neutron precursors that decay partly outside the core, off-gas xenon removal, an intermediate salt loop, and a freeze plug that can drain fuel salt into a passively cooled drain tank.

The PWR plant model includes CVCS charging/letdown and boration, reactor-coolant-pump seal failure, ECCS with HHSI/LHSI/accumulators/RHR, RWST and containment-sump suction, a dynamic pressurizer with PORV/safety-valves/PRT, a representative steam generator with three-element feedwater control, AFW trains, main feedwater and condensate pumps, regenerative feedwater heaters, SG tube plugging/rupture, MSIVs/ADVs/condenser bypass, staged turbine-generator/load rejection, EDGs, batteries, switchyard undervoltage relays, CCW/SW, large-dry containment sprays and fan coolers, and spent-fuel-pool cooling. The RBMK branch has its own two-loop Main Circulation Circuit, per-loop drum control, DREG-backed electrical supply and turbo-generator rundown, split ECCS, Accident Localization System suppression pool, graphite-gas and CPS-cooling auxiliaries, main feedwater pumps, and pressure-tube-break fault. The MSR branch uses the MSRE's air-cooled radiator and blower rather than a turbine, with freeze protection, off-gas xenon removal, a sealed reactor cell, and online redox/corrosion chemistry.

The reactor protection system runs scram and warning channels in parallel: high local flux, short positive period, low DNBR for water-cooled cores, high coolant outlet temperature, pressurizer pressure and level, steam-generator level, containment pressure, actual reactor coolant flow, turbine overspeed, seal LOCA, manual scram, and loss of off-site power, plus warning-only channels such as ORM, flow excursion, SI/RWST/NPSH, CVCS, EDG, battery, grid/vital-AC, CCW/SW, AFW, MFW, SGTR, secondary valves, containment spray/temperature, and spent-fuel-pool state. The annunciator panel latches channels until reset. The reactivity stack decomposes rods, boron, Doppler, moderator, void, xenon, and burnup effects as signed live contributions, so power maneuvers visibly turn into xenon and temperature-coefficient problems rather than magic numbers.

## Faults and procedures

The plant is presented as an interactive schematic: clicking any component opens an inspector with that component's readouts, controls, and — where applicable — fault injection. PWR failures include a stuck-open PORV, closed AFW discharge MOVs, loss of component/service water, loss of spent-fuel-pool cooling, EDG failure, main-feedwater trip, SGTR, load rejection, turbine-governor failure, RCP trips, and loop isolation. The RBMK inspectors expose loss of offsite power, diesel failure, MCP/loop actions, main-feedwater and auxiliary-cooling trips, manual ECCS, and a pressure-tube break. The MSR inspectors expose fuel-pump and off-gas loss, freeze-heater/plug actions, radiator/blower controls, and redox control. These are command-level injections rather than scripted replays: you choose the failure and watch the coupled plant respond.

The PWR procedure action bundles live in the reactor-core inspector and collect the immediate actions of a response procedure into one button. E-0 scrams the reactor and forces AFW/SI. E-1 closes the PORV block valve, fires SI, and disables pressurizer heaters. E-1.3 aligns sump recirculation and RHR. The SBO action starts the EDGs, sheds vital loads, starts AFW, and verifies the turbine-driven AFW block valve open. Spent-fuel-pool cooling restoration and diverse makeup are operated from the spent-fuel-pool inspector. The guided-scenario engine currently ships two objective-tracked exercises — PWR load following and MSR overpower — and already supports scripted events for future replay scenarios.

## Accessibility

The toolbar, the left-rail controls, and every open component inspector are keyboard-operable; sliders accept arrow keys and Home/End, and buttons are reachable in tab order. The scram action is bound to Shift+S regardless of focus, and the time-acceleration control has standard keyboard navigation. Selecting a component on the plant schematic currently uses a pointer (wheel to zoom, drag to pan); keyboard selection of schematic components is a known gap. Color-coded displays -- fuel temperature, coolant temperature, reactivity sign -- are also disambiguated by position and numeric value. The toolbar theme control switches between light and dark presentations. ARIA labels are applied to the major interactive controls and graphical readouts. Known hazards: the plant schematic, trend plots, and readouts animate while the simulator is running, and newly latched trips blink briefly before going solid. Use pause or single-step to stop motion.
